Who are we?

"All things begin with an idea." - Earl Nightingale

Oracle Corporation is a large international computer technology company based in the United States, Austin, Texas.
Oracle was founded in 1977 by Larry Ellison along with his partners Bob Miner and Ed Oates under the name SDL, or its full name "Software Development Laboratories."

What about the name?

Software Development Laboratories later changed its name to "Relational Software, Inc" in 1979 and then again to "Oracle Systems Corporation" in 1983 to better align with its flagship product, Oracle Database.
A few years later (1989), Oracle moved its headquarters to Redwood Shores near the city of Redwood City, California, and finally changed its name to Oracle Corporation (as we know it today).

We like business

Part of Oracle's success was due to the use of the C programming language, which is supported by various operating systems and is easy to manipulate and adapt to other systems, Oracle continued its path to success by acquiring several companies, including PeopleSoft, BEA Systems, and others. In an attempt to compete with Amazon Web Services and its products.

Oracle even went as far as announcing a partnership with former rival Microsoft which included a direct connection between the two companies, allowing customers to store data on both cloud computing systems and run applications on either Oracle or Azure.

Later, Oracle announced its move to Austin, Texas (where it is located today), and a year later made several interesting business moves, including the acquisition of Cerner and the subsequent purchase of Federos, an AI and automation tool for improving communication performance, which cost $28.3 billion in cash.

Oracle offers a wide range of products including database software, cloud services, enterprise software, EPM, and SCM.
